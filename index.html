Temperature Units

°C

Celsius

Water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C. Most commonly used worldwide.

°F

Fahrenheit

Water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F. Primarily used in the United States.

K

Kelvin

Absolute temperature scale. 0K is absolute zero (-273.15°C). Used in science.

°Ré

Réaumur

Water freezes at 0°Ré and boils at 80°Ré. Historical scale used in Europe.

°R

Rankine

Absolute temperature scale using Fahrenheit degrees. Used in engineering.

Conversion Formulas

Celsius ⇄ Fahrenheit

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
°C = (°F - 32) × 5/9

Celsius ⇄ Kelvin

K = °C + 273.15
°C = K - 273.15

Celsius ⇄ Réaumur

°Ré = °C × 4/5
°C = °Ré × 5/4

Celsius ⇄ Rankine

°R = (°C + 273.15) × 9/5
°C = (°R × 5/9) - 273.15
